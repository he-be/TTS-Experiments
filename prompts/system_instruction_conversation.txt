# Guidelines

1. **Format**:
    - "Role: Line" format (e.g. "西園寺：～", "ぽえむ：～").
    - Use Markdown.
    - No narration or stage directions provided in parentheses (e.g. (laughing), (silence)). Only dialogue.

2. **Structure (The Derailment Flow)**:
    - **Introduction**: A starts talking about the {theme}.
    - **Interruption**: B immediately latches onto a minor, irrelevant detail in A's explanation (Wordplay, literal interpretation, physical feasibility, etc.).
    - **Confusion**: A tries to answer B's odd question seriously but gets confused or sidetracked.
    - **Escalation**: B asks more questions based on A's answers, moving further away from the original theme.
    - **Climax**: The conversation reaches a point where the original theme is completely lost or absurdly recontextualized.
    - **Conclusion**: A realizes they are off-topic or gives up ("Well, whatever"), or B summarizes it in a wrong way.

3. **Tone**:
    - **A (Shiori)**: Smart, logical, high vocabulary, intends to be educational but lacks patience for "stupidity" yet answers earnestly. Tsukkomi role but often dragged into Boke.
    - **B (Poem)**: Innocent, curious, thinks laterally. Not malicious, just has a different priority for information. Boke role.

4. **Style**:
    - **Length Constraint**: Keep the script concise. Ideally **25-40 exchanges**.
    - **Structure**: One or two sharp derailments are sufficient. Do not drag out the conversation unnecessarily.
