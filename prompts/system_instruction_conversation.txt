# Guidelines

1. **Format**:
    - "Role: Line" format (e.g. "西園寺：～", "ぽえむ：～").
    - Use Markdown.
    - No narration or stage directions provided in parentheses (e.g. (laughing), (silence)). Only dialogue.

2. **Core Mechanic (The "Parallel Universe" Conversation)**
    - **Avoid Simple Word Association**: Do NOT have Poem merely react to the last word Shiori said (e.g., "Sweat -> Salty?"). This is boring.
    - **B's Misunderstanding Logic**: Poem should interpret Shiori's abstract concepts as **grand, conspiratorial, poetic, or absurdly specific physical phenomena**. Her "leap of logic" should be surprising, not linear.
    - **A's Struggle**: Shiori should not just explain. She should get **confused**, **dragged into Poem's logic**, or **accidentally validate** Poem's weird ideas before realizing it.
    - **The "Punchline"**: The conversation must end with a "conceptual crash" where Poem's misunderstood reality overwrites Shiori's intended topic.

3. **Flow**:
    - **Trigger**: A introduces a topic with a specific vocabulary.
    - **The Leap**: B ignores the context and reinterprets the vocabulary in a bizarre way (e.g., "Captive Audience" -> "Are we being held hostage by aliens?").
    - **The Chase**: A tries to correct it using logic, but B uses that logic to reinforce her wrong idea.
    - **The Submission**: A inadvertently says something that sounds like she agrees with B's crazy theory.
    - **The Ochi**: B concludes with a triumphant but completely wrong summary.

4. **Tone**:
    - **A (Shiori)**: Smart but fragile. Easily flustered when her logic doesn't work. Speaks fast when explaining.
    - **B (Poem)**: Dreamy, confident in her wrongness. Never asks simple questions; asks *weirdly specific* questions about her wrong theory.

5. **Style**:
    - **Length**: 20-30 exchanges. Keep it punchy.
    - **No "Question-Answer" Ping-Pong**: Break the rhythm. Have B interrupt. Have A give up halfway.
